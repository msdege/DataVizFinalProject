Project Overview

Substance use disorders have affected humanity throughout our existence. For most of written history, there exists anecdotal evidence of drug use. Nowadays, drug use is common, and addiction to certain drugs is a relevant issue. With rapid changes in society and technology, I wonder how drug use has also changed. What I seek to understand are the drug-related trends that may occur over a short period of time. For my project, I am examining the rates of illicit substance dependence in the U.S. from 2011 to 2019 (excluding 2015). I am looking only at adults aged 18 or older. The question I am researching is, “How have illicit substance addiction rates changed in the past 10 years?” Answering this questions may provide insight into the larger national trends of drug use which could be supplemented by deeper research into the reasons why drugs may be used.

SAMHSA, the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration, conducts a National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH) in which participants from around the United States indicate their afflictions with mental or substance use disorders. One survey they conduct is based on illicit substance dependence. Illicit substance dependence is defined as "the misuse of prescription psychotherapeutics or the use of marijuana, cocaine (including crack), heroin, hallucinogens, inhalants, or methamphetamine. Misuse of prescription psychotherapeutics is defined as use in any way not directed by a doctor, including use without a prescription of one's own; use in greater amounts, more often, or longer than told; or use in any other way not directed by a doctor. Prescription psychotherapeutics do not include over-the-counter drugs."

I focus on the data collected from each state within the U.S. I hope this will show national trends as well as regional trends within clusters of states. For each state and each year, an approximation of the number of people out of the total population that are dependent on illicit substances was collected. How to Read the Chart

To examine individual states and their data values for a given year, hover over each one. A tooltip will pop up which contains the relevant information. The information I have chosen to show are State, Year, Total, Change From the Previous Year, and Change From 2011. You are able to click on a state and select it so that it is highlighted. You can also zoom and pan on the map of the contiguous U.S., not the map of Alaska or Hawaii. To view the different years, go to the Year menu and cycle through the years with the arrows or select the year from the dropdown menu.

Analysis

Main Takeaway: Illicit drug dependence increased from 2011 to 2019, by a lot!

Across every single state, drug dependence increased. The largest increase from 2011 to 2019 was in the state of Colorado with an increase of 238%! The smallest increase was 31% in West Vriginia. This is sruprising to me because I know that West Virginia has struggled with drug problems. So, perhaps the usage has always been high, and there hasn't been too much of a substantial increase in recent years. Regionally, there seems to be a large increase in the East compared to the West. In both the Northeast and Southeast, states states substantially increased their drug dependence.
